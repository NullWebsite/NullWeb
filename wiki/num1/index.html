How to Make Alternate Websites

This tutorial shows you how to create websites that are literally exact copies of other sites. Think of it like a proxy—but a bit trickier. The hard work will pay off though. Ready? Let’s go.

Step 1: Register on afraid.org

Head over to afraid.org and sign up or log in. Then go to the domain registry section.

Step 2: Find an Unblocked Domain

Scroll to the bottom where it says “Page 1 of ...” and type the number after “of” to jump to the last page. Press Enter. Then press Ctrl+F and search for “public.” If it says “0 of 0,” click “Last page” manually. Otherwise, copy the first domain with “public” in the row (for example, nylme.xyz).

Open a new tab (Ctrl+T), paste (Ctrl+V) that domain, and hit Enter. If the site isn’t blocked, you’ll be able to continue.

Step 3: Find the Target Website's IP Address

Go to NSLookup. Enter the URL of the website you want to clone (e.g., www.nullmedia.infinityfreeapp.com), hover over one of the IPv4 addresses, and click the clipboard icon.

Go back to afraid.org, paste the IP address into the “Destination” box.

Step 4: Customize Your Subdomain

In the “Subdomain” box, type whatever you want your alternate site to be called. For example, if you want nullweb.nylme.xyz to redirect to Google, type google. Complete the captcha, click Save, then wait about an hour.

After that, test your new alternate website. If all went well, it should work like a charm!
